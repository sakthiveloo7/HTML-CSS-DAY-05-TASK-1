Kazi Nazrul Islam
The national poet of Bangladesh

Rebel Poet

Kazi Nazrul Islam is the national poet of Bangladesh especially recognized as a Bidrohi Kobi(rebel poet) around the country. Nazrul is a famous poet, musician and philosopher of Bangladesh. The famous poet Nazrul's writing deal with the revolution of that period of british goverment. His revolutionary attempt helped him to be a national poet of Bangladesh. He has raised his voice against any kind of fascism and opperession and got popularity swiftly around the indian subcontinent. Nazrul Islam reached the peak of his fame in 1922 with Bidrohi (The Rebel), which remains his most famous work, winning the admiration of India's literary society for his description of a rebel. Nazrul Islam started a bi-weekly magazine, Dhumketu ("ধূমকেতু", 'Comet') on 12 August 1922 that was critical of the British Empire.

Awesome Writer

Nazrul's writings explored themes such as love, freedom, humanity and revolution. He opposed all forms of bigotry and fundamendalism, including religious, caste-based and gnder-based. Throughout his career, Nazrul wrote short stories, novels, and essay but is best known for his songs and poems. he pioneerd new music forms such as Bengali ghazals. Nazrul wrote and composed music for nearly 4,000 songs(many recorded on HMV and gramophone records), collectively known as Nazrul Geeti.
"I raise my head beyond this world and, high, ever erect and alone" - Kazi Nazrul Islam
